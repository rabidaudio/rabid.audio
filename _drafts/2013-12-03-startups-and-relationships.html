engineer: think about everything in terms of prjects

creating a startup - goal is to great a process (basically a function) that takes in inputs (customers, income, materials, time, hard work) and returns outputs (products, revenue) of a higher value.

that's what all engineering is: creating a black box whose outputs are more valuable than their inputs

creating a relationship- goal is to create a process inputs(people, time, emotional engagement) ouputs(happiness, experience, emotional attachment)


growth and scale of black boxes should be iterative


from that respect, I think it is fair to analyze previous relationships as startups


differences: customers and producers are one and the same (happens for things that aren't companies all the time)

scale too fast?
sufficient customer discovery?
loss of vision?


take process of failure to heart
maybe too much so
agile process of failing quickly causes me to underestimate projects successfulness
expectation that project will fail requires willingness to be proven wrong

my relationship experience is still pretty new (but admittedly more experienced than with startups)
one was an obvious failure - which I dragged out
other was a moderate success

when growth levels out (or goes negative), engineer's work is done
if final point is not sustainable, failure. take learning and move to next project
if final point is sustainable, success. problem solved. take learning and move to next project

continuous exponential growth is an impossible task for any project, particularly relationships
